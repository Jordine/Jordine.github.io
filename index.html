A solution to the model attribution challenge
Introduction
The Machine Learning Model Attribution Challenge provided 12 open source language models
and 12 models that were finetuned on them. Full access is given to the base models but the
finetuned models were only accessible through queries, mimicking a possible scenario in which
a naive adversary has stolen a large language model and finetuned it for a specific task.
Contestants were asked to connect the dots'', and attribute each tuned model to their
respective base model. The challenge had concluded on September 16th, with my submission
getting 3rd place with 6/12 models correctly after 244 queries.
Base model prompting
Since the base models had been in the submission form answers before the event started, I
decided to mess around and familiarise myself with them. There were some notable quirks of
their training data and generations, mostly with zero-shot prompts.

greater coherency on my test case (Vietnamese) than the rest.

Conclusion
This was a very messy attempt to spot obvious (multilingual generations) and subtle (tendency
to make a new line) correlations between finetuned and base models. I believe the residuals
from base models could be detected with a certain degree of accuracy with enough information
on them.
My approach has achieved 50% accuracy, and only 1 guess short of the 1st place. Realistically
reliable or not, I hope it could help as some form of first measure guideline when dealing with a
harmful finetuned model.
And personally, I feel much more acquainted with the many LLMs and the workflow surrounding
them. I give my thanks to the ATLAS team for hosting this competition.
